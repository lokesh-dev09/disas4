About the Project

Understanding the technology behind Malaysia's disaster risk prediction and monitoring system.

Project Overview

The Malaysia Disaster Risk Monitor is an advanced platform that leverages machine learning and data science to analyze historical patterns of natural disasters in Malaysia, specifically focusing on floods, earthquakes, tsunamis, and forest fires.

Our system compiles and analyzes data from authoritative sources like NASA Earthdata, EM-DAT: The International Disaster Database, and the WorldRiskReport, combining this with real-time satellite imagery to identify both current risk areas and predict future high-risk zones.

Our Mission

To provide accurate, timely, and accessible disaster risk information to help communities, government agencies, and relief organizations better prepare for and respond to natural disasters in Malaysia.

Our Vision

To reduce the impact of natural disasters through advanced prediction and early warning systems, ultimately saving lives and minimizing economic losses across Malaysia.

Risk Assessment Methodology

Our risk assessment methodology combines historical data analysis with machine learning algorithms to identify patterns and predict future disaster risks. The system assigns risk levels from 1 (very low risk) to 5 (life-threatening) based on various factors specific to each disaster type.

Risk Level Color Code Description Example Criteria
Level 1 Green Very Low Risk Minimal historical incidents, favorable conditions
Level 2 Light Green Low Risk Few historical incidents, generally safe conditions
Level 3 Yellow Moderate Risk Occasional incidents, some risk factors present
Level 4 Red High Risk Frequent incidents, multiple risk factors
Level 5 Black Life-Threatening Earthquake > 6.0 magnitude, Tsunami depth > 101cm
Disaster-Specific Criteria
Floods
  • Level 3: Affecting 5,000-10,000 people
  • Level 4: Affecting 10,001-50,000 people
  • Level 5: Affecting >50,000 people or depth >3m
Earthquakes
  • Level 3: Magnitude 4.0-4.9
  • Level 4: Magnitude 5.0-5.9
  • Level 5: Magnitude >6.0
Tsunamis
  • Level 3: Inundation depth 31-60cm
  • Level 4: Inundation depth 61-100cm
  • Level 5: Inundation depth >101cm
Forest Fires
  • Level 3: 100-300 hectares affected
  • Level 4: 301-1,000 hectares affected
  • Level 5: >1,000 hectares affected
